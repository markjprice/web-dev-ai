> **Prompt**: Show how viewBox and width/height interact using multiple examples with resizing.

To understand how `viewBox` and `width`/`height` interact, think of it as a **painting (viewBox)** inside a **picture frame (width/height)**. The `viewBox` defines the coordinates of the drawing, while the `width` and `height` define how much space that drawing occupies on your webpage.

---

## 1. The "Perfect Fit" (1:1 Ratio)
When the aspect ratio of the `viewBox` matches the aspect ratio of the `width` and `height`, the image fills the space exactly as you drew it.

**The Code:**
```html
<svg width="200" height="200" viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <rect x="0" y="0" width="100" height="100" fill="blue" />
</svg>
```
* **Internal Map:** 100x100 units.
* **Screen Size:** 200x200 pixels.
* **Result:** The blue square is scaled up by 2x to fill the 200px frame perfectly.

---

## 2. The "Zoom In" Effect
If you make the `viewBox` smaller than the actual drawing coordinates, you are effectively "cropping" into the image.

**The Code:**
```html
<svg width="200" height="200" viewBox="25 25 50 50">
  <rect x="0" y="0" width="100" height="100" fill="red" />
</svg>
```
* **The Logic:** We told the SVG to start at point $(25, 25)$ and only show a $50 \times 50$ area. 
* **Result:** Even though the rectangle is 100 units wide, the browser zooms into the center. You only see the middle portion of the red square, stretched to fill the 200px frame.



---

## 3. The "Letterbox" (Mismatched Aspect Ratio)
By default, SVG preserves its aspect ratio (similar to `object-fit: contain` in CSS). If your frame is a different shape than your drawing, the browser adds "empty space."

**The Code:**
```html
<svg width="400" height="200" viewBox="0 0 100 100">
  <circle cx="50" cy="50" r="50" fill="green" />
</svg>
```
* **The Logic:** The drawing is a square ($100 \times 100$), but the frame is a wide rectangle ($400 \times 200$).
* **Result:** The circle will stay a perfect circle and sit in the middle of the 400px width. It will not stretch into an oval because the browser protects the aspect ratio.

---

## 4. Forced Stretching (`preserveAspectRatio`)
If you actually *want* the SVG to distort and fill the entire width and height regardless of shape, you have to disable the default behavior.

**The Code:**
```html
<svg width="400" height="200" viewBox="0 0 100 100" preserveAspectRatio="none">
  <circle cx="50" cy="50" r="50" fill="orange" />
</svg>
```
* **Result:** The circle will be stretched horizontally into a wide oval to fill the entire $400 \times 200$ area.



---

## Summary Checklist
1.  **`width/height`** are your CSS-level dimensions (the "Frame").
2.  **`viewBox`** is your internal coordinate system (the "Canvas").
3.  If the aspect ratios match, the image scales cleanly.
4.  If the aspect ratios **don't** match, the browser "Letterboxes" the image unless you use `preserveAspectRatio="none"`.
