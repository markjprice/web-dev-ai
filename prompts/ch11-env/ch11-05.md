> **Prompt:** Please explain how token limits and context windows affect AI coding assistants when working with a large Django codebase.

In a large Django codebase, the **context window** is the AI’s "working memory"—the total number of tokens (words or code fragments) it can process in a single turn. While 2026-era models like **GPT-5.2 Codex** and **Gemini 3 Pro** boast windows from 272K to over 1M tokens, the "effective" limit is often much lower due to how Django projects are structured.

---

## 1. The "Lost in the Middle" Phenomenon

Research in 2026 shows that even with a 1M token window, AI performance degrades when critical information is buried in the middle of a large file.

* **Django Impact:** If you provide a 2,000-line `models.py` and a 1,500-line `views.py`, and the crucial `ForeignKey` relationship is defined in the middle of the models file, the AI may "forget" it exists while writing a view.
* **The Risk:** The AI might hallucinate a field name or suggest an invalid join because it lost focus on the schema definition tucked in the "middle" of its context.

---

## 2. Token Bloat in Django’s Architecture

Django is an "explicit" framework. A single request/response cycle often touches several files: `settings.py`, `urls.py`, `models.py`, `serializers.py`, and `views.py`.

* **Cumulative Cost:** Loading these five files into an AI assistant can easily consume 20,000 to 50,000 tokens.
* **The "Context Rot" Problem:** As you chat with the AI, the conversation history grows. Every time you ask "Wait, fix the serializer too," the AI re-processes the entire file history. Eventually, older context—like the original business requirements—gets pushed out of the "active memory" to make room for new code blocks.

---

## 3. Effective Strategies for 2026

To manage a large Django project within these limits, elite teams have moved away from "pasting the whole file" to **Context Engineering**.

### Selective Scoping (The @-Reference)

Modern tools like **Cursor** and **Claude Code** allow you to "pin" only the relevant parts of the codebase.

* **Instead of:** `@codebase, fix the login.`
* **Better:** `@models.py (User class) @serializers.py (LoginSerializer) fix the password validation.`
* **Why:** This reduces the "noise-to-signal" ratio, ensuring the AI's attention is focused on the actual logic.

### Structural Modularization

The "monolithic" Django file is an AI's worst enemy.

* **The Fix:** Split large `views.py` into a `views/` directory with separate files per feature.
* **Benefit:** Smaller files (under 500 lines) are processed with nearly 100% accuracy, as they fit comfortably within the "high-attention" zones at the start and end of the context window.

## 5. Summary: The "Needle in a Haystack"

Even with the massive windows of 2026, the **Needle in a Haystack** problem remains. If you provide a million tokens but don't specify which "needle" (the specific Django model) is relevant, the AI will provide a generic answer. **Precision in what you include is always more valuable than the total volume of code you provide.**
